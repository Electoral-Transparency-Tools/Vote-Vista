=== THEME ===
Six pillars: Anna (food), Abhaya (security), Akshara (education),
Arogya (health), Abhivruddhi (development), Aadaaya (income).

=== KEY PROMISES ===
Food / welfare:
- 'Atal Aahara Kendra' in every municipal-corporation ward for affordable, healthy food.
- 'Poshana' scheme: 0.5 L Nandini milk daily + 5 kg Shri Anna (millets) monthly to every BPL household.
- 3 free cooking-gas cylinders/year to BPL families (Yugadi, Ganesh Chaturthi, Deepavali).
- Free ration for BPL households.

Health:
- 'Mission Swasthya Karnataka': one Namma Clinic with diagnostics in every municipal ward.
- Free annual master health check-up for senior citizens.

Education / youth:
- 'Visvesvaraya Vidya Yojane': upgrade govt schools via partnerships with eminent individuals/institutions.
- 'Samanvaya' scheme: link SMEs and ITIs for education-employment ecosystem.
- Financial incentives for IAS/KAS/Banking/Govt-jobs coaching.
- 10 lakh manufacturing-sector jobs.

Women / social justice:
- Fixed-deposit scheme for SC/ST women.
- Viability-gap revolving fund (Rs 500 crore) for industrial clusters and women-led enterprises.

Bengaluru-specific (relevant to CV Raman Nagar):
- 'State Capital Region' tag for Bengaluru.
- Karnataka Residents' Welfare Consultative Committee to reform the Karnataka Apartment
  Ownership Act, 1972 and modernise grievance redressal (apartment dwellers' ease of living).

Governance / identity:
- Implement Uniform Civil Code (UCC) based on a high-level committee.
- Introduce NRC; expedite deportation of illegal immigrants.
- 'K-SWIFT' wing against religious fundamentalism and terror.

Farmers / economy / tourism:
- Rs 30,000 crore fund for micro cold storage + agro-processing units in every gram panchayat.
- Rs 1,500 crore plan for tourism circuits/corridors.
- 10 lakh housing sites for the homeless; EV hub push.

=== CONTEXT FOR COMPARISON ===
BJP LOST the 2023 Karnataka election statewide (Congress formed govt). Therefore most
state-level manifesto promises were NOT in BJP's power to implement post-2023. For the
CV Raman Nagar seat, the relevant promise-vs-result analysis is at the MLA/constituency
level (local works, MLA-LAD funds) rather than statewide policy delivery.
